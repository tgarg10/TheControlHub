Why Automate Agriculture?

As Singapore lies one degree north of the equator, we only have one season all year round. Being a highly-populated and land-scarce urban city with a population of 5.7 million and only 724 sq km of land, less than 1% of our land is available for farming. While both local and imported produce are important to Singapore‚Äôs food security, we are aiming to produce 30% of our nutritional needs by 2030 to reduce our reliance on imports.

What does Automating Agriculture mean?

Seeds are irradiated using UV-C radiation to improve the harvest quality. Process of sowing seeds is automated using a moving device that uses Infrared Radiation (IR). Saplings are grown using red and blue LEDs. Soil moisture levels are also monitored in order to calculate the adequate amount of water required by the plant to grow, making irrigation process more effective and eliminating over-irrigation. Harvested plants are irradiated using UV-C. Integrated Smart Manual Control over the entire farm.
